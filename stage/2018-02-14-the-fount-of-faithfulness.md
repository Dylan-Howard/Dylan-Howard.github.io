<p class="quote">To the saints who are in Ephesus, and are faithful in Christ Jesus</p>
<p class="quote">Ephesians 1:1b</p>
<hr>
<p>Paul addresses his letter much like his others, but here he address this group (the only others being the church in Colossae)  as “faithful.” Each of us ought to long for this description to be true of our own character.</p>
<p>Before we can be truly faithful, we must understand where faithfulness originates. Faithfulness to God flows from Christ alone. The attribute, “faithful,” is rightly followed with, “in Christ.” As saints, we have been purchased by the blood of Christ. God purified us with His blood (Hebrews 1:3; 2:14), gave us a new heart with new affections (Ezekiel 36:26), upholds us with His Holy Spirit (Ezekiel 36:27), and carries our purification to completion (Hebrews 12:2). From beginning into eternity, our faithfulness is dependent on God.</p>
<p>Recognizing this inspires a humility in us, but, in the next breath, inspires confidence. We recognize our weakness to be faithful, but we cling to the promises of an ever-faithful God. Christ taught, “those who are well have no need of a physician, but those who are sick. I came not to call the righteous, but sinners” (Mark 2:17). In our unfaithfulness, we rejoice that Christ is our faithful physician. He came not for those who facade as faithful, but those who humble recognize their weakness. This gives humility of character and confidence in God bids us to approach the throne of Grace for continuation of our faithfulness (Hebrews 4:16).</p>
<p>So let us strive for faithfulness as we continually run to the throne of our faithful God from whom all faithfulness flows.</p>
