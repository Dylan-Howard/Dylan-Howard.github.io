<p class="quote">…In Your presence, there is fullness of joy;…</p>
<p class="quote">Psalm 16:11</p>
<p>O’ how easy it is to forget this promise. I neglected it so often. I let my heart be Lord. I begin to seek satisfaction in worldly promises. I let half-pleasures fill my affections and I am left devoid of life and barren of joy. My vain attempts at fulfillment and rest vanish in a vapor. The vanity of my pursuits is revealed and I am left bare, raw, and empty.</p>
<p>O’ how good it is that God does not leave us this way. He is tender. He cares for His little weak ones. “In Your presence,” the Psalmist proclaims. To what effort of theirs is this owed? None at all. How could these unholy ones incline themselves to enter the presence of the holy God? No way at all, except the cleansing power of Christ’s atonement.</p>
<p>And what are the fruits of the Lord’s presence? The psalmist continues, “there is fullness of joy.” Not partial joy—not like the empty or half-pleasures of the worldly—but full joy. By His gracious gift alone, we can call on God to save us. By His merit alone, we can gladly approach the Throne of Grace. Now—and, much more, soon will—we gather around His throne as the elect of God—His precious bride—and joyfully savor His fullness (Ephesians 1:23). There is no action of those in His presence; they are only beneficiaries of His proximity. By grace, they are glad. This gladness is not in the merger offers of the world—though they may appear attractive—but in Christ, who offers an infinitely greater choice.</p>
