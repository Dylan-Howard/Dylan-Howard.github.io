Performance optimization is often where "Always a Learner" meets "Senior Engineering." I inherited a legacy sync suite where individual queries were taking 20-30 minutes, with the entire suite bottlenecking at 2 hours.

## The Problem: O(n²) Thinking in an O(m) World

The legacy system was pulling massive datasets to compare state, leading to high CPU spikes and astronomical egress stats. To solve this, I shifted the strategy to a **timestamp-based delta detection** model.

## Technical Strategies:

- **`O(m)` Deletion Detection:** Rather than comparing full tables, I implemented a strategy to detect deleted records in linear time based on change tracking.
- **SQL Short-Circuiting:** Utilized `NOLOCK` and `SELECT 1` shortcuts to allow for rapid-fire status checks without locking production tables.
- **Statistical Extrapolation:** Since granular telemetry was sparse, I used manual load testing and statistical modeling to predict scaling behavior across the school district.

```sql
-- Optimized delta fetch with non-blocking reads
SELECT TOP (1000) [Id], [Payload]
FROM [IdentitySync].[Queue] WITH (NOLOCK)
WHERE [LastModified] > @LastSyncTimestamp
  AND [IsProcessed] = 0
OPTION (RECOMPILE); -- Optimize for specific parameter sniffing at scale
```

## The Results

We moved from 15-minute polling intervals to 1-minute high-frequency polls. This wasn't just a technical win; it was a win for _User Empathy_. Staff and students no longer had to wait hours for their access changes to propagate—it happened in near real-time.
