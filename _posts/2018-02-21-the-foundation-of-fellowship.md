<p class="quote">Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ</p>
<p class="quote">Ephesians 1:2</p>
<hr>
<p>Paul's greeting to his brothers and sisters is striking. He roots their relationship in the grace and peace of God. He greets them with "grace"-the favor of God shown to underserving and unlovely adulterers-and "peace"-the fullness of delight supplied by reconciliation with God. Furthermore, Paul notes that both grace and peace flow from God alone.</p>
<p>I was once asked by a non-believer that if I could keep the fellowship and community of the church without Christ, would I take it? I was befuddled. I had never thought of what community without Christ would be. What he didn't realize, and I labored to explain, was the necessity of Christ in unifying His body, the Church. Our unity is not natural, but supernatural.</p>
<p>In this greeting, Paul recognizes-and teaches here-that the foundation of our fellowship is Christ, the Cornerstone. We enjoy our earthly relationships, but none are as sweet as the communion of the saints.</p>
<p>Thus, there's an important diagnostic for our relationships: if Christ were removed, what would happen to our relationships? Would our groups of friends change? Would we continue on normally, or is, in large part, our fellowship with those who we have nothing in common but Christ. Christ's body is diverse, so we ought not to seclude ourselves to one extremity, but enjoy the blessing of the fullness of Christ's body.</p>
