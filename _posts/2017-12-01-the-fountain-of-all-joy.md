<p class="quote">"Delight yourself in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart."</p>
<p class="quote">Psalm 37:4</p>
<hr>
<p>Think of the commands of the Bible. Repent. Go. Teach. Declare. There are several others, but how often do we consider the imperative, "Delight". Many entirely disregard this command. Go, teach, declare, but don’t dare delight, praise, or rejoice. (c.f. Psalm 16:11, Philippians 4:4) Why would we do this? If the greatest pleasure we can experience is in God, why wouldn’t we seek pleasure in Him? Wouldn’t this leave us unfulfilled? If we followed all other commands, but failed to delight in God, wouldn’t we have severed our emotions from our actions, our spirit from our body? So, why would we not delight in Him?</p>
<p>Likely, because we are not naturally inclined to love God. People of darkness prefer darkness; illumination is unpleasant to them. The command to delight in God only reveals our decayed hearts—cold and dead from sin. In our own power, we are unable to stir up the affections needed to truly delight in God. We reject this imperative because it undermines our own glory, but this does not remove our need for a new heart with God-centered desires.</p>
<p>The beauty of the gospel is that, in Christ, God fulfilled this command to delight and now gives us new hearts to do the same—hearts that love Him, treasure Him, adore Him, praise Him as the only Almighty Eternal God who is worthy of all praise from all people of all generations. (Ezekiel 36:26, Jeremiah 32:38-41, Philippians 2:5-11) Though we failed to keep this command God completed it. Though we are weak, God empowers us to follow it. Our desire are no longer dead, cold desires. They are lively and God-centered. By grace, delight in God.</p>
<hr>
<p class="quote">FOUNTAIN OF ALL JOY,</p>
<p class="quote">You, Yourself, delight in Your infinite perfection. In Your joy of Your glory, You have made us this same way. Our greatest satisfaction is found in You alone. Despite this, we have turned from Your glory to promote our own. We have sinned against Your infinite worthiness and separated ourselves from the satisfaction of Your presence.</p>
<p class="quote">Praise be to You that, in and for Your joy and glory, You have given us new hearts to love, adore, and treasure You as supreme. Let us grow in our affections for You and know the infinite goodness and pleasures You offer. Let these affections result in love for our neighbors, and vigilant effort for Your Kingdom.</p>
<p class="quote">All glory be to You.</p>
