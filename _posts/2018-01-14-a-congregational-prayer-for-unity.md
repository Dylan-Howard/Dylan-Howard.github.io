<h4>A Congregational Prayer for Unity</h4>
<hr>
<p class="quote">I therefore, a prisoner for the Lord, urge you to walk in a manner worthy of the calling to which you have been called, with all humility and gentleness, with patience, bearing with one another in love, eager to maintain the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace.</p>
<p class="quote">Ephesians 4:1-3</p>
<hr>
<p class="quote">GOD OVER ALL,</p>
<p class="quote">You reign in heaven. In Your holiness, You created all things in unity with You and with others. In our wickedness, we hated Your authority. In Your greatness, for Your glory, You bore our curse to offer us Your righteousness. We rejoice in this news of Your goodness that You’ve made available to us.</p>
<p class="quote">When You called us to Yourself, You breathed life into our hearts, and sealed us for redemption. We are adopted as a child of the Father, betrothed as the bride of Christ, built up as the temple of the Spirit, by Your grace, we have the hope of being brought into the perfect and eternal union with You, our Triune God. Let us, an embodiment of Your Church, display this unity.</p>
<p class="quote">It is You who works in us both to will and work for Your good pleasure (Philippians 2:13). Let us remember our dependence on Your power and, in this humble condition, gently work to edify our body, teaching one another Your precious truths, reproving one another in false doctrines, correcting one another in righteousness, that we may be pure and blameless at Your return.</p>
<p class="quote">Let us carry out this labor with all patience and perseverance. Let us remember that You alone give the growth, as we faithfully and trustingly labor for Your kingdom. In our brokenness, we’re quick to division, but, by Your power, let us be eager to maintain the unity we have in You. We ask these things in Your mighty name, for the display of Your glory through us, to the world, Amen.</p>
