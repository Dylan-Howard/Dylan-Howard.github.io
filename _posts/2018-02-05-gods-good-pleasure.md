<h2>God’s Good Pleasure</h2>
<p class="quote">Do good to Zion in your good pleasure;</p>
<p class="quote">build up the walls of Jerusalem;</p>
<p class="quote">Psalm 51:18</p>
<hr>
<p>In this prayer of repentance, David has already asked God to clean his heart (Psalm 51:7,10a), give him heavenly desires (Psalm 51:12a), and uphold him with a willing spirit (Psalm 51:12b). In this verse, He asks again, “Lord keep me from my wicked desires.” David rightly understands that fire must be fought with fire—that the evil desires of his heart can only be extinguished with the superior pleasures of God.</p>
<p class="quote">“in your presence there is fullness of joy;</p>
<p class="quote">at your right hand are pleasures forevermore.”</p>
<p class="quote">Psalm 16:11</p>
<p>Indeed, our temporal joys are microscopic in comparison to the full and forever joys of Christ (who is at the right hand). The joy of Christ is more than enough to choke out any wayward desire.</p>
<p>Even more, God does this “in [His] good pleasure”. He doesn’t unwillingly or begrudgingly tune our hearts to His; He delights in this! David’s plea is not met with a stern reluctance; it is met with an exceedingly joyful work! God takes delight in His children delighting in Him. This is our great hope, that the Supreme of the universe is pleased to make His creation pleased with Him.</p>
